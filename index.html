ABOUT Me

I am Aryan S. Mishra, currently pursuing a Master of Engineering in Robotics at the University of Maryland, College Park. My work spans Deep Learning, Robotics, Computer Vision, and Language Processing. I completed my undergraduate studies in Electronics and Communication Engineering at VIT Vellore, India.

I have significant experience in deploying projects using GANs, LSTMs, and segmentation-based neural networks. As a robotics major, I am skilled in designing, modeling, and controlling robots, as well as deploying perception and computer vision pipelines and ROS2 software architectures. My projects include GAN deployment, MRI data segmentation, ARIAC module efficiency, and stereo vision pipelines.

At the Tubaldi Lab under Dr. Eleonora Tubaldi, I have contributed to two key projects:

Automatic Segmentation of the Aorta
- Deployed neural networks like V-NET, U-NET, SSL, and Uncertainty-Informed Prototype Consistency Learning.
- Achieved accuracies of 95%, 94.75%, 93%, and 97% respectively.

Characterizing the Tactile Gripping Action of a Soft Robotic Gripper
Goal: Detect the shape,size and characteristics of the object that the gripper is picking up only on the basis of the pressure volume curve based on how much it inflates and deflates.
- Set up experiments including manufacturing grippers and 3D printing object bases.
- Utilized Harvard Apparatus and linear stages with MATLAB and LabVIEW.
- Designed a PCB circuit for pressure, servo motor, and connector circuit.
- Collected and analyzed data to predict object characteristics using classification and regression algorithms.
- Worked with a UR-3e robot for the experiments.

My role as a Robotics Intern at Aditya Aluminium showcased my ability to optimize industrial processes and innovate robotic solutions.

My ambition is to impact medical imaging and robotics through deep learning and computer vision. My journey reflects a commitment to learning, innovation, and solving practical challenges. I aim to contribute to teams leveraging the latest in robotics and AI, driven by a passion for technology.

Experience

Machine Learning Researcher

Tubaldi Lab, University of Maryland

Since January 2024, my role as a Machine Learning Researcher at Tubaldi Lab, University of Maryland, has immersed me in cutting-edge research at the intersection of machine learning and medical imaging. One of my key projects involves the precise segmentation of the Aorta, employing advanced models such as V-NET, U-NET, SSL, and Uncertainty-informed Prototype Consistency Learning. This work is crucial for enhancing the accuracy of medical diagnostics, and through rigorous development and testing, I achieved impressive accuracies of 95%, 94.75%, 93%, and 97% respectively. This project not only demonstrates my ability to navigate complex machine learning landscapes but also highlights my contribution to potentially life-saving medical research. In addition to my work in medical imaging, I have also been instrumental in the development and optimization of an advanced 3D-printed robotic gripper for UR-3E robots. This innovative project leverages predictive capabilities to assess object pressure, volume, and dimensions, thereby enhancing the robot's handling efficiency and adaptability. Currently, under construction and training, this robotic gripper stands as a testament to my skills in marrying machine learning with practical robotics applications. The project embodies my commitment to pushing the boundaries of what is possible in robotics, aiming to create more intelligent, capable, and efficient robotic systems.

In summary, my involvement in the development of the circuitry for the Octagonal Static Thrust Test Pad and Flight Recovery System was marked by meticulous attention to detail, innovative problem-solving, and a steadfast commitment to safety, all of which significantly contributed to the success of these high-powered rocketry applications.
